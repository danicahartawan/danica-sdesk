system — Monet
voice → structure → memory


Monet app preview



Monet is a voice-driven thinking system that captures unstructured thought and transforms it into persistent structure.

Spoken input is parsed into key ideas, organized into linked nodes, and maintained as a live knowledge graph. The system operates as an external layer of memory—continuously updating without requiring manual organization.

Monet is self-hosted and designed for ongoing use, where thoughts accumulate and connect over time rather than being stored as isolated notes.














artifact — The Leisure Journal
writing without outcome



The Leisure Journal removes prompts, timelines, and productivity constraints from writing.

There is no structure to follow and no expectation of output. The absence of guidance is intentional—creating space for thought that is not directed, optimized, or evaluated.

It functions as a counter-system to productivity tools, prioritizing presence over capture.

tool — Nudge
behavior without interruption



Nudge is a lightweight behavioral layer that introduces small prompts throughout the day.

Rather than requiring active input, it operates in the background—surfacing suggestions at moments of low friction. The system is designed to influence action subtly, without becoming a task manager or notification stream.

Nudge focuses on timing and placement rather than volume, reducing reliance on explicit reminders.
